When designing interiors for West Texas, the climate presents unique challenges and opportunities. The region's arid conditions, dramatic temperature swings, and intense sunlight require thoughtful material selection and design strategies.

## Understanding the Climate

West Texas is characterized by:
- Low humidity and arid conditions
- Extreme temperature variations between day and night
- Intense, direct sunlight
- Occasional dust storms
- Strong winds

## Material Selection

### Flooring
- **Natural stone**: Excellent thermal mass that helps regulate temperature
- **Polished concrete**: Durable and reflects heat
- **Wide-plank hardwood**: Adds warmth while maintaining breathability

### Walls
- **Lime plaster**: Breathable and regulates humidity
- **White oak paneling**: Natural warmth with excellent durability
- **Adobe-style finishes**: Traditional and climate-appropriate

## Design Strategies

### Thermal Mass
Incorporate materials with high thermal mass like stone and concrete to absorb heat during the day and release it slowly at night.

### Natural Ventilation
Design spaces to promote cross-ventilation, taking advantage of evening breezes to cool interiors naturally.

### Shading Solutions
- Deep overhangs protect windows from direct sun
- Interior light filters diffuse harsh light
- Strategic window placement maximizes indirect light

## Color Palette

Light, reflective colors help bounce light and reduce heat absorption while maintaining the warm, welcoming aesthetic that defines West Texas design.

## Conclusion

By understanding and working with the local climate, we create spaces that are not only beautiful but also comfortable and sustainable year-round.
