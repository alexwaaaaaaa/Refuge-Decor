# Brand Guidelines: Refuge Decor & Designs LLC

## Brand Personality

### Core Attributes
- **Warm**: Inviting, approachable, human-centered
- **Quiet**: Understated, confident through restraint
- **Organic**: Natural, authentic, grounded in materials
- **Architectural**: Respectful of structure, space, and light
- **Minimal**: Essential elements only, intentional simplicity
- **Handcrafted**: Artisan quality, attention to detail
- **Timeless**: Transcends trends, ages gracefully
- **Luxury Without Excess**: Premium through quality, not ostentation

### Brand Voice
- **Tone**: Confident but humble, knowledgeable but accessible
- **Language**: Simple words, sophisticated ideas
- **Perspective**: We guide, we don't dictate. We collaborate.
- **Personality**: Like a thoughtful friend who is an expert designer

### Brand Promise
We create spaces that feel like a refuge—thoughtfully designed environments that honor both the land and the people who inhabit them.

## Design Philosophy

### Core Principles
1. **Design Should Disappear**: Great design becomes invisible in daily life
2. **Materials Tell Stories**: Every material has history, character, and purpose
3. **Light Defines Space**: Natural light is our primary design medium
4. **Less Is More**: Each element must earn its place
5. **Context Is King**: Design must respond to place, climate, and culture
6. **Comfort Is Non-Negotiable**: Beauty without livability is failure
7. **Sustainability Is Luxury**: Quality materials and craftsmanship last generations

### West Texas Ranch Aesthetic
- **Respect for Landscape**: Design that complements, not competes with, the land
- **Indoor-Outdoor Connection**: Blurring boundaries between interior and exterior
- **Raw Materials**: Stone, wood, metal, leather in their natural states
- **Earthy Palette**: Colors drawn from the Texas landscape
- **Architectural Honesty**: Structure and materials visible and celebrated
- **Modern Comfort**: Contemporary livability within traditional forms

### Anti-Principles (What We Are Not)
- Not trendy or fashion-driven
- Not corporate or institutional
- Not ostentatious or showy
- Not cold or sterile
- Not generic or placeless
- Not complicated or confusing
- Not wasteful or disposable

## Photography Direction

### Overall Style
- **Natural Light**: No harsh artificial lighting, embrace shadows and depth
- **Authentic Moments**: Spaces that look lived-in, not staged
- **Architectural Emphasis**: Show structure, materials, and spatial relationships
- **Human Scale**: Include people to show scale and livability
- **Detail Shots**: Close-ups of materials, textures, and craftsmanship

### Composition Guidelines
- **Wide Shots**: Establish space and context
- **Medium Shots**: Show room relationships and flow
- **Detail Shots**: Highlight materials and craftsmanship
- **Lifestyle Shots**: People using spaces naturally
- **Process Shots**: Show work in progress (behind-the-scenes authenticity)

### Technical Specifications
- **Format**: High-resolution RAW files, deliver as WebP and JPEG
- **Aspect Ratio**: 3:2 for horizontal, 2:3 for vertical, 1:1 for details
- **Color Profile**: sRGB for web, Adobe RGB for print
- **File Size**: Optimized for web (under 500KB per image), full resolution available for press
- **Naming Convention**: project-room-description-date (e.g., marfa-ranch-living-main-2024)

### Photography Requirements by Project Type

**Residential Projects**
- Exterior: Front facade, rear facade, landscape context
- Interior: Each major room, 2-3 angles per room
- Details: Custom furniture, lighting, materials, hardware
- Lifestyle: Family using spaces, entertaining, daily life

**Ranch Projects**
- Landscape: Aerial views, land context, outbuildings
- Exterior: Main house, guest houses, barns, outdoor living areas
- Interior: Public rooms, private rooms, transitional spaces
- Details: Stone work, wood beams, metal work, leather

**Staging Projects**
- Before: Empty or dated space (document starting point)
- After: Staged space (same angles for comparison)
- Details: Key staging elements, furniture, accessories
- Lifestyle: Potential buyers imagining themselves in space

## Lighting Philosophy

### Natural Light Strategy
- **Maximize Daylight**: Design to capture and distribute natural light
- **Embrace Seasons**: Acknowledge changing light throughout year
- **Shadow Play**: Use shadows for depth and drama
- **Golden Hour**: Leverage warm morning and evening light
- **Directional Light**: Understand how light moves through spaces

### Artificial Light Strategy
- **Layered Lighting**: Ambient, task, and accent lighting
- **Warm Color Temperature**: 2700K-3000K for residential warmth
- **Dimmable Everything**: Control light levels for mood and function
- **Hidden Sources**: Fixtures that provide light without being visible
- **Statement Fixtures**: Select fixtures that are sculptural objects

### Photography Lighting
- **No Flash**: Rely on natural light whenever possible
- **Golden Hour**: Schedule shoots for optimal natural light
- **Overcast Days**: Soft, diffused light for interior shots
- **Reflectors**: Bounce natural light to fill shadows
- **Long Exposures**: Capture natural light in low-light situations

## Materials Language

### Primary Materials
- **Stone**: Local limestone, sandstone, slate (left natural or lightly honed)
- **Wood**: Oak, walnut, mesquite (local species, natural finish)
- **Metal**: Wrought iron, brass, copper (patina preferred over polished)
- **Leather**: Full-grain, vegetable-tanned (ages beautifully)
- **Textiles**: Linen, wool, cotton (natural fibers, neutral colors)
- **Concrete**: Polished or exposed aggregate (architectural applications)

### Material Philosophy
- **Honesty**: Materials should look like what they are
- **Aging**: Materials should patina and improve with time
- **Local Sourcing**: Use materials from the region when possible
- **Sustainability**: Choose durable, renewable, non-toxic materials
- **Craftsmanship**: Celebrate joinery, construction, and making

### Material Combinations
- **Stone + Wood**: Classic ranch pairing, warm and grounded
- **Metal + Leather**: Industrial refinement, masculine elegance
- **Concrete + Wood**: Modern warmth, architectural simplicity
- **Textile + Metal**: Softness and structure, balance of textures

### Materials to Avoid
- **Plastic**: No synthetic materials in primary applications
- **Laminate**: No fake materials or imitations
- **High-Gloss**: No reflective surfaces (except where functional)
- **Exotic Species**: No endangered or non-local woods
- **Trendy Finishes**: No colors or patterns that will date quickly

## Texture Language

### Texture Philosophy
- **Tactile Quality**: Every surface should invite touch
- **Visual Depth**: Texture creates visual interest without color
- **Natural Variation**: Embrace imperfection and variation
- **Layering**: Combine textures for richness without clutter

### Primary Textures
- **Rough Stone**: Natural, uneven, ancient
- **Smooth Wood**: Hand-rubbed, not plastic-smooth
- **Woven Textiles**: Visible weave, natural fiber
- **Hammered Metal**: Tool marks, artisan quality
- **Plaster**: Subtle variation, hand-troweled
- **Leather**: Grain visible, patina over time

### Texture Combinations
- **Rough + Smooth**: Contrast creates visual interest
- **Matte + Sheen**: Subtle variation, not high contrast
- **Hard + Soft**: Balance of rigidity and comfort
- **Natural + Refined**: Raw materials with precise execution

## Icon Philosophy

### Icon Style
- **Line-Based**: Clean, simple line icons (not filled)
- **Minimal**: Essential elements only, no decoration
- **Consistent**: Same stroke weight, same visual language
- **Custom**: Drawn specifically for brand, not stock icons
- **Purposeful**: Every icon must have clear meaning

### Icon Usage
- **Navigation**: Simple, recognizable symbols
- **Actions**: Clear indication of interactive elements
- **Features**: Visual representation of services or benefits
- **Social Media**: Brand-consistent social icons

### Icon Guidelines
- **Stroke Weight**: 2px consistent stroke
- **Corner Radius**: Slight rounding (2px-4px) for warmth
- **Size**: 24px standard, scalable grid system
- **Color**: Neutral gray (#666) default, brand color on hover
- **Spacing**: Generous padding around icons

## Illustration Philosophy

### Illustration Style
- **Architectural**: Plan drawings, elevations, sections
- **Hand-Drawn**: Subtle imperfection, human touch
- **Minimal**: Line drawings with limited color
- **Purposeful**: Illustrations must explain, not decorate

### Illustration Usage
- **Process Diagrams**: Show design process step-by-step
- **Floor Plans**: Architectural drawings of spaces
- **Material Boards**: Visual representation of material selections
- **Before/After**: Illustrated transformations for staging projects

### Illustration Guidelines
- **Line Weight**: Consistent with icon style (2px)
- **Color**: Limited palette (brand colors + neutrals)
- **Style**: Architectural, technical but accessible
- **Scale**: Clear scale indicators, human figures for reference

## Spacing Philosophy

### Spacing Principles
- **Generous White Space**: Luxury requires breathing room
- **Consistent Rhythm**: Repeating spatial patterns create harmony
- **Hierarchy**: More space around important elements
- **Grouping**: Related elements close together, unrelated far apart

### Spacing Scale
- **Base Unit**: 8px (1 unit)
- **Small**: 8px, 16px (1-2 units)
- **Medium**: 24px, 32px (3-4 units)
- **Large**: 48px, 64px (6-8 units)
- **Extra Large**: 96px, 128px (12-16 units)

### Spacing Applications
- **Text Padding**: 16px-24px around body text
- **Section Margins**: 96px-128px between sections
- **Card Spacing**: 24px-32px internal padding
- **Grid Gaps**: 24px-32px between grid items
- **Button Padding**: 16px-24px vertical, 32px-48px horizontal

## Color Philosophy

### Color Strategy
- **Neutral Foundation**: Warm grays and earth tones as base
- **Accent Color**: Single accent color for CTAs and highlights
- **Contextual Colors**: Colors drawn from West Texas landscape
- **Limited Palette**: Restrict to 5-7 colors total
- **Accessibility**: Ensure 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text

### Primary Palette
- **Neutral Dark**: #2A2A2A (near black, warmer than pure black)
- **Neutral Medium**: #666666 (warm gray)
- **Neutral Light**: #E8E4DE (warm off-white)
- **Neutral White**: #FAF8F5 (cream white, not pure white)
- **Accent**: #8B7355 (warm brown, like leather or wood)

### Secondary Palette (Contextual)
- **Terracotta**: #C17B5F (like Texas clay)
- **Sage**: #8B9A7D (like desert plants)
- **Slate**: #5A6A7A (like West Texas rock)
- **Sand**: #D4C4A8 (like desert sand)

### Color Usage Rules
- **Backgrounds**: Neutral white or light gray only
- **Text**: Neutral dark or medium for readability
- **Accents**: Use sparingly for CTAs and highlights
- **Photography**: No color overlays, let images speak
- **Hover States**: Accent color on hover, neutral default

### Color Accessibility
- **Text on Light**: Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- **Text on Dark**: Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- **Large Text**: Minimum 3:1 contrast ratio
- **Interactive Elements**: Clear focus states with color change
- **Color Blindness**: Never rely on color alone to convey meaning

## Typography Philosophy

### Typography Strategy
- **Serif for Headings**: Warm, traditional, authoritative
- **Sans-Serif for Body**: Clean, modern, readable
- **Limited Fonts**: Maximum 2 font families
- **Web-Safe Priority**: Use fonts that load quickly
- **Scale System**: Consistent type scale based on modular scale

### Primary Typeface Pairing
- **Headings**: Playfair Display (serif) - warm, elegant, traditional
- **Body**: Inter (sans-serif) - clean, modern, highly readable
- **Alternative Headings**: Cormorant Garamond (more architectural)
- **Alternative Body**: Source Sans Pro (more humanist)

### Type Scale (Modular Scale: Major Third 1.25)
- **H1**: 48px (3rem) - Page titles, hero headlines
- **H2**: 38px (2.375rem) - Section titles
- **H3**: 30px (1.875rem) - Subsection titles
- **H4**: 24px (1.5rem) - Card titles, feature headings
- **Body Large**: 18px (1.125rem) - Lead paragraphs, emphasized text
- **Body**: 16px (1rem) - Body text, standard content
- **Body Small**: 14px (0.875rem) - Captions, metadata, secondary text

### Typography Rules
- **Line Height**: 1.5 for body text, 1.2 for headings
- **Letter Spacing**: -0.02em for headings, 0 for body
- **Paragraph Spacing**: 24px between paragraphs
- **Max Width**: 65-75 characters per line for body text
- **Font Weight**: 400 for body, 600 for headings, 700 for emphasis

### Typography Hierarchy
1. **H1**: One per page, primary message
2. **H2**: One per section, secondary message
3. **H3**: Multiple per section, tertiary organization
4. **H4**: Component-level organization
5. **Body**: Content, information, stories
6. **Small**: Metadata, captions, secondary information

## Interaction Philosophy

### Interaction Principles
- **Predictable**: Users should know what will happen before they click
- **Responsive**: Every interaction should provide immediate feedback
- **Forgiving**: Easy to undo or correct mistakes
- **Accessible**: Keyboard and screen reader friendly
- **Subtle**: Animations should enhance, not distract

### Interaction Patterns
- **Hover**: Subtle color change, slight scale (1.05), smooth transition (300ms)
- **Focus**: Clear outline (2px solid accent color), offset (2px)
- **Active**: Subtle scale (0.98), immediate feedback
- **Loading**: Simple spinner or progress bar, no complex animations
- **Error**: Clear error message in red, inline with form field

### Animation Philosophy
- **Purpose-Driven**: Every animation must have a reason
- **Subtle**: Motion should be barely noticeable
- **Fast**: 200-400ms duration, no slow animations
- **Natural**: Use easing curves that feel physical (ease-out)
- **Respectful**: Honor reduced motion preferences

### Micro-Interactions
- **Button Hover**: Color change + subtle lift
- **Link Hover**: Underline animation (left to right)
- **Card Hover**: Subtle shadow increase + slight lift
- **Image Hover**: Subtle zoom (1.1) + dark overlay
- **Form Focus**: Border color change + label animation

### Scroll Behavior
- **Smooth Scroll**: Smooth scrolling for anchor links
- **Scroll Animations**: Elements fade in as they enter viewport
- **Parallax**: Subtle parallax on hero images only
- **Scroll Progress**: Minimal progress indicator (optional)
- **Back to Top**: Subtle button that appears after scrolling
